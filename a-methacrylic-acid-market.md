<p><p>The α-Methacrylic Acid market is primarily driven by two production methods: the Acetone Cyanohydrin Method and the Isobutylene Oxidation Method. The Acetone Cyanohydrin Method involves the reaction of acetone with hydrogen cyanide, producing high yields of α-Methacrylic Acid suitable for various applications. In contrast, the Isobutylene Oxidation Method utilizes isobutylene and oxygen, offering a more environmentally friendly process with reduced waste. Both methods cater to diverse industrial sectors, including coatings, adhesives, and plastics, influencing market growth and dynamics.</p></p>
