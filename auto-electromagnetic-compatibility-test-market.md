<p><strong>The Auto Electromagnetic Compatibility Test Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Bulk Current Injection (BCI)</li><li>Conducted Emissions (CE)</li><li>Conducted Transient Emissions (CTE)</li><li>Conducted Transient Immunity (CTI)</li><li>Electrostatic Discharge (ESD)</li><li>Radiated Emissions (RE)</li><li>Radiated Immunity (RI)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Auto Electromagnetic Compatibility Test market encompasses various testing types to assess vehicles' electromagnetic performance. Bulk Current Injection (BCI) evaluates immunity to high-frequency noise, while Conducted Emissions (CE) measures unwanted emissions on power lines. Conducted Transient Emissions (CTE) examines short-duration voltage spikes, and Conducted Transient Immunity (CTI) tests resilience to such disturbances. Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) assesses susceptibility to static electricity, whereas Radiated Emissions (RE) tests emissions into the environment. Radiated Immunity (RI) checks resistance to external electromagnetic fields, ensuring reliable vehicle operation.</p></p>
