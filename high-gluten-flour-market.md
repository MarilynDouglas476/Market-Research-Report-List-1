<p><p>The high-gluten flour market primarily includes two types: machine-milled flour and stone-ground flour. Machine-milled flour is produced using modern industrial techniques that ensure consistency and high uniformity, making it suitable for commercial baking and products requiring strong gluten, such as bagels and artisan bread. In contrast, stone-ground flour is milled using traditional methods, retaining more nutrients and flavor, appealing to consumers seeking artisanal or whole grain options. Both types cater to different segments of the baking industry and consumer preferences.</p></p>
