<p><p>Bio-pharmaceutical logistics encompasses the transportation and storage of temperature-sensitive products, primarily vaccines, biologics, and pharmaceuticals. Cold chain logistics involves managing a consistent, controlled temperature environment, ensuring product integrity from manufacturing to delivery. This is critical for items that require refrigeration. Conversely, non-cold chain logistics pertains to products that can be stored and transported at room temperature, simplifying supply chain processes. Both types are essential for maintaining the efficacy and safety of bio-pharmaceutical products throughout their lifecycle.</p></p>
