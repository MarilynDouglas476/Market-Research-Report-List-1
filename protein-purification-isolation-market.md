<p><p>Protein purification involves isolating specific proteins from complex mixtures for research, diagnostics, or therapeutic use. Key market types include ultrafiltration, which separates proteins based on size; precipitation, which uses solubility differences to isolate proteins; and centrifugation, which separates components by density. Chromatography, a widely used technique, separates proteins based on various interactions, while electrophoresis utilizes electric fields for separation based on charge and size. Other methods encompass techniques like dialysis and affinity purification, enhancing protein isolation efficiency.</p></p>
