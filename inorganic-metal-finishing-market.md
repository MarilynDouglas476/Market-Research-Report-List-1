<p><p>The inorganic metal finishing market comprises various types, each serving distinct purposes. Anodizing enhances corrosion resistance and durability by converting metal surfaces into protective oxides. Cladding involves bonding a layer of metal onto a substrate for improved properties. Conversion coatings provide a chemical layer that enhances adhesion and corrosion resistance. Electroplating deposits metal onto a surface using electrical currents, while electroless plating does so chemically, offering uniform thickness. Other methods include various surface treatments tailored for specific applications.</p></p>
