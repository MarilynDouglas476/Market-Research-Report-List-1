<p><p>Overhead and underground conductors are essential components of electrical transmission and distribution systems. Overhead conductors are typically mounted on poles or towers, facilitating the transmission of electricity across long distances. They are easy to install and maintain, making them a popular choice in rural and suburban areas. Underground conductors, on the other hand, are buried below the surface and are widely used in urban settings where aesthetics and space constraints are factors. They offer enhanced reliability and reduced risk of weather-related disruptions.</p><p>The Overhead and Underground Conductor Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.2% during the forecast period, driven by factors such as increasing demand for electricity, infrastructure development, and the growing trend of smart grid technologies. Additionally, the push for renewable energy sources is leading to more investments in both overhead and underground installations. The market is witnessing a shift towards integrated solutions that provide enhanced performance, reliability, and sustainability, aligning with global initiatives for energy efficiency and carbon reduction. As urbanization and industrialization continue to rise, the demand for effective and reliable conductor systems is expected to strengthen significantly.</p></p>
